who am i

Hello

From the Central Coast of California to the South of France; from the bike saddle to the tennis equipment market; and from hosting a radio show to manning a wine shop’s digital marketing strategy, I’ve always stretched between improbable places and industries.

I’m not sure what impact it had on me. But I do know why I’ve looked for those weirdly diverse experiences. I’m curious, for better or worse. I research the crap out of random subjects for no reason. Especially when it’s about the three things I love the most, music, wine and food, in that order.

Being dropped into new places where I had to figure stuff out all over again worsened my obsession for understanding humans – people-watching is more entertaining to me than most Netflix shows, and I eavesdrop slightly more than is considered as reasonably creepy.

But I don’t watch, hear and analyze people just for the sake of it. I want to understand their problems, see trends, imagine solutions to them. And my first instinct when I think is to draw, to color, to find a way to story-tell what I have in mind visually. I got an Industrial Design degree in college, and have been working in visual design since, because it’s so consistent with way I think.

This gives you a decently accurate picture of who I am, and a good reason for why I’m here, as a recently graduated UX designer from General Assembly. I’d say the top three things I’m most skilled at are product design, design strategy and concept generation. Figuring stuff out as I go and multitasking are not a problem for me. They’re how I work. Other than that, I’m flexible; more than happy to play on a team or fly solo. I prefer fast-paced environments, but I can dance to more than one tune and rhythm.
